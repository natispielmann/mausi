The material

Vegetable tanned leather.

Not all leather is the same. Veg tan is a different thing entirely — slower, more honest, more alive.

⏳

Time, not chemicals

Veg tan uses plant-based tannins — oak bark, quebracho, chestnut. The process takes weeks, not hours. Chrome tanning uses heavy metals and takes 24 hours. Veg tan leather is denser, firmer, infinitely more responsive to the hand.

🌿

Traceable by nature

I know the tanneries I work with by name — what tannins they use, where they source their hides. You cannot rush a natural tannin and expect a good result.

✨

The patina — a life lived

Veg tan darkens where you touch it most. It softens at the fold. It records sunlight, rain, the oils of your hand. A veg tan piece carried for ten years is not worn out — it is finished. This is not ageing. This is memory.

♻️

End of life

Veg tan leather is biodegradable. Chrome-tanned is not — heavy metals make it hazardous waste. When a Mausi piece reaches the end of its life — decades from now — it returns to the earth.

"A veg tan piece does not compete in speed.
It competes in time."

The maker

Mausi.

Leather artisan · Empoli, Italy

I am Argentine-German, based in Italy. I came here to pursue my dream of becoming a bespoke shoemaker — and fell in love with the whole world of Italian leather craft: the tanneries, the tools, the slowness, the pride of a well-made thing.

I trained under mentors in shoemaking, shoe design and leather goods. I came second place in the Veg Tan leather design contest. I graduated from Schola in Florence. I have made shoes, bags, sculptural leather objects, furniture upholstery. I work without patterns when the piece calls for it.

Mausi is not my business name. It is my name — the most personal thing I could put on a piece of leather.
